FAQ

Question Answer
What is the main goal of your website? Our website aims to reorganize and present the FBI's top 10 Most Wanted Fugitives in a more structured and visually appealing manner, making it easier for the public and stakeholders to identify and be on the lookout for these fugitives.
Who benefits from this platform? The primary beneficiaries are the FBI, the general public, news channels, and even criminals. Our platform ensures that vital information is disseminated efficiently and effectively, thereby aiding in the swift capture of wanted criminals.
Why did you choose the FBI Wanted API? We were keen on understanding and showcasing the top ten most wanted criminals. The FBI Wanted API provides this specific data, and we believe that by presenting it in a refined manner, we can assist in the apprehension of these individuals.
What technologies are you using to develop the website? We're utilizing Visual Studio Code for front-end development with HTML, CSS, and Javascript. For API requests, we're using tools like Insomnia and Postman.
Is the website mobile-responsive? Yes, our platform is designed to be accessible and user-friendly across various devices, including smartphones, computers, tablets, and TVs.
How does the platform contribute to catching fugitives faster? By presenting the most wanted list in a coherent and engaging manner, citizens are better informed and can be more vigilant. This increased awareness can lead to faster identification and capture of these fugitives.
How will you present the importance of your platform to the public? We plan to conduct a detailed showcase or presentation, demonstrating the different features and utilities of our platform, explaining its significance in aiding the FBI and ensuring public safety.
Where can I access the original FBI Wanted list? The original list can be accessed on the FBI's official website at FBI's Top Ten Most Wanted Fugitives.
